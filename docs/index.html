I.
The parameter argument

Don't flatten a photo.

Same output (32 channels of 224×224), two architectures. The MLP weights every pixel-position independently and pays for it. The convolution reuses one small kernel at every location.

✕ MLP · Linear(150528, 32)

Flat-MLP, 1st layer.

4.82M
params = (3 × 224 × 224) × 32 + 32 = 4,816,928

Every pixel position has its own weights. No notion that pixel (10,10) sits next to (10,11). A sleeve in the top-left and a sleeve in the bottom-right are different features.

✓ CNN · Conv2d(3, 32, 3)

Conv layer, same channels.

896
params = 32 × (3 × 3 × 3) + 32 = 896

One 3×3 kernel slides over the whole image. ~5,000× smaller — and the same edge-detector finds an edge in the top-left and the bottom-right. Translation invariance for free.

II.
The mechanism

A 3×3 kernel sees.

Pick a kernel. Nine weights slide across the image, multiplying and summing pixels under the window. Edges, blurs, ridges — all from one tiny grid. A trained CNN learns these kernels rather than guessing them.

Choose a kernel

Each output pixel = sum of (kernel weight × input pixel) over the 3×3 window. The CNN learns whatever values fit the task; you are previewing what trained kernels look like.

Left: synthetic 24×12 input "image" (vertical bands + diagonal). Right: same image after the kernel slides across it. Try Sobel-X and watch vertical edges light up.

III.
A strategy

Three paths through transfer learning.

How much data do you have? How close is your domain to ImageNet? Those two questions pick the path.

Feature extraction

Freeze the entire pretrained backbone. Train only a fresh classifier head on top. Cheap, fast, robust — the default starting move.

When · close domain · small data

Fine-tune last block

Unfreeze the last conv block (e.g. layer4) and the head; keep early generic edge/texture filters frozen. Tiny lr (1e-4).

When · medium domain gap

Train from scratch

Build a CNN and learn every weight. Only viable with lots of data; otherwise overfits hard. Even then, pretrained is usually a strong baseline.

When · 10K+ per class
IV.
A checklist

Three checks before "transfer learning didn't work."

Most "transfer learning lost to a from-scratch CNN" stories are preprocessing bugs.

1

How much data?

Under ~1K per class? Start pretrained. 10K+ per class? From-scratch becomes viable; pretrained is still a strong baseline.

✕ "500 photos / class · from-scratch."✓ "500 / class · ResNet18 head-only."
2

Domain gap?

Colour natural photos? Head-only will lift you. Grayscale, X-rays, satellite? Budget for fine-tuning the last conv block.

✕ "Head-only on X-rays."✓ "Unfreeze layer4 · lr 1e-4."
3

Preprocessing contract?

ImageNet normalisation, 3-channel RGB, 224×224 input. Skip any and you're measuring preprocessing bugs, not the model.

✕ "Grayscale · raw 0–255 · 64×64."✓ "RGB · ImageNet stats · 224×224."

V.
Check yourself

Eight questions from the field notebook.

Click to flip. Numbering matches lesson.md.

▸ Click to revealTwo reasons not to flatten a 224×224 colour photo into an MLP.
Parameter count: 150,528 inputs × 256 hidden ≈ 38M weights in one layer.
No spatial prior: the MLP treats every pixel as independent. It has no notion that adjacent pixels are related; a sleeve in the top-left and one in the bottom-right are unrelated features.
▸ Click to revealKernel arithmetic: Conv2d(3, 32, 3) params vs MLP equivalent.
Conv: 32 × (3 × 3 × 3) + 32 = 896. MLP: 150,528 × 32 + 32 ≈ 4.82M. ~5,000× smaller — the same kernel is reused at every spatial location.
▸ Click to revealStride 2, padding 1 (kernel 3), 2×2 max-pool — output size effect?
Stride 2 halves H/W. Padding 1 with kernel 3 keeps output the same size. 2×2 max-pool halves H/W and keeps the strongest activation in each 2×2 block.
▸ Click to revealTinyCNN on 1×28×28: shape into the first Linear?
Conv 1: 16×28×28 → Pool: 16×14×14 → Conv 2: 32×14×14 → Pool: 32×7×7 → Flatten: 32×7×7 = 1,568.
▸ Click to reveal"CNN 88% vs MLP 87% — tiny gain, new framework." Correct them.
Framework is unchanged — same five-line training step from L07. Conv2d/MaxPool2d drop in for Linear. Fashion-MNIST is easy; on CIFAR-10 the CNN beats the MLP by 10+ pp. Improvement scales with image complexity.
▸ Click to reveal500 colour studio photos per class, 12 categories. (a) scratch, (b) head-only, (c) fine-tune?
(b) head-only is the right first move; (c) is a good second. 500/class is too few to train from scratch. Studio clothing photos are close to ImageNet, so the pretrained backbone transfers directly. If you need more, unfreeze layer4 with lr=1e-4.
▸ Click to revealHead-only loses on Fashion-MNIST but wins on CIFAR-10. Why?
Fashion-MNIST is grayscale 28×28 silhouettes; CIFAR-10 is colour 32×32 natural photos — close to ImageNet. When the pretrained domain is far from yours, unfreeze the last conv block with a tiny lr (1e-4); deepest features adapt while early generic filters stay intact.
▸ Click to revealResNet18 + 64×64 grayscale + new head → dismal accuracy. Three preprocessing checks?
(1) Channels: ResNet expects 3-channel RGB; repeat grayscale to 3 channels. (2) Normalisation: apply ImageNet stats (mean=[.485,.456,.406]). (3) Input size: ResNet expects ~224×224; resize up before normalising.
